## 4 · Grammar concept: です as "is / am / are"

Japanese sentences often end with です. It turns a noun into a polite statement of identity or description.

```
[Noun] + です。 → "It is [Noun]" / "I am [Noun]"
```

- 先生です。 — "I am a teacher."
- 本です。 — "It is a book."
- はい、そうです。 — "Yes, that's right."

Note: です sounds more like "des" in speech — the final `u` is often nearly silent.

Full page: [grammar/desu-copula.md](./grammar/desu-copula.md).

## 6 · Speaking practice

Imagine someone just held a door for you. What do you say?
Now imagine you bumped into them. What do you say?

<details><summary>Suggested answers</summary>

- Door held → ありがとうございます (thanks) — or すみません, ありがとうございます (acknowledging the trouble + thanks).
- Bumping into them → すみません.

</details>

## 7 · Quiz

1. How would you say "thank you" politely?
2. What does すみません mean? (Give two meanings.)
3. True or false: です is a Japanese noun.
4. Pronounce aloud: し, ち, つ. What sounds do they make?

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. ありがとうございます
2. "Excuse me" / "Sorry" / "Thanks (for the trouble)." (Any two.)
3. False — it's the polite copula ("is / am / are").
4. **shi**, **chi**, **tsu** (not "si, ti, tu").

</details>
