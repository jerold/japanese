# Graded Readers & Manga

Reading practice in phase 1 should be cheap and frequent — short, kana-heavy material that matches your current level.

## Absolute beginner reading (days 5–30)

You can start reading *something* in Japanese as soon as you know hiragana. Don't wait for more kanji.

### Tadoku graded readers (tadoku.org)

Free PDF mini-books graded by difficulty. Level 0 uses only hiragana and tiny vocab; each level up adds more kanji and sentence complexity. The single best first reading resource for phase 1.

### NHK News Web Easy

News articles rewritten in simpler Japanese, with furigana over every kanji. Most articles are ~200 words. Not quite phase 1 level, but begin tasting it late in phase 1.

### Children's books

Kana-only or furigana-over-kanji children's books are excellent. Nosy Crow's "Japanese for Busy People" reader, or any picture book with kana. Public library Japan sections often have these.

## Early manga to consider

Manga with furigana over all kanji are called "小学生向け" (for elementary schoolers) — perfect for learners.

- **よつばと！ (Yotsuba&!)** — slice-of-life, short sentences, full furigana, adorable.
- **ドラえもん (Doraemon)** — short, episodic, full furigana, simple humor.
- **しろくまカフェ (Polar Bear Café)** — pun-heavy but slow-paced.
- **はなまるおべんとう** or other cooking/daily-life manga.

Avoid in phase 1: Shounen Jump titles (no furigana in most), heavy fantasy (specialized vocab), slang-heavy high school comedies.

## How to read as a beginner

1. **First pass** — read for general flow. Don't look up words.
2. **Second pass** — underline words you kept missing. Look up 3–5, not 30.
3. **Third pass** — read aloud if you can.
4. Move on. Don't finish-hunt.

## Pacing

One Yotsuba chapter (~25 pages) takes a beginner 45–90 min. That's fine. Your 15-minute daily lesson can include 3 pages of manga instead of a listening clip on some days.

## Tools that help without spoiling the effort

- **Yomichan / Yomitan** (browser extension) — hover over any Japanese word on a web page for an instant dictionary pop-up. Life-changing for web reading.
- **Jisho.org** — the beginner-friendly Japanese dictionary.
- **ichi.moe** — pastes a sentence, returns a breakdown. Use sparingly; it can become a crutch.
