# Shadowing Technique

Shadowing means repeating speech aloud with minimal delay behind the speaker — like you're "shadowing" them. It is one of the highest-leverage single practices for building fluency, especially for pronunciation, pitch, and automaticity.

## The basic method

1. Choose a short audio clip (30 seconds to 2 minutes) at or slightly below your current level. Needs a transcript available.
2. **Listen once** with no text. Just hear it.
3. **Read + listen** — follow along with the transcript while listening.
4. **Parrot**: play the audio and repeat each sentence in the pause between lines.
5. **Shadow**: play the audio continuously, and speak along with it, about half a beat behind the speaker.
6. Repeat 4–5 times over a few sessions until it flows.

## Key rules

- **Speak aloud.** Whispering or mouthing doesn't build muscle memory. Close a door if you need to.
- **Match rhythm first, accuracy second.** Get the beat before the exact sounds.
- **Don't chase perfection.** 70% right and flowing beats 100% right and halting.
- **Short clips, many passes.** A 45-second clip you shadow 20 times teaches more than a 10-minute clip you shadow once.

## Source material

Great sources for shadowing practice:
- JapanesePod101 dialogs (come with native audio + transcripts).
- NHK Easy News (audio + text).
- Anime scenes with Japanese subs (see [anime-with-subs-approach.md](./anime-with-subs-approach.md)).
- Yuko's *Speak Japanese Naturally* YouTube videos — subtitled and short.

Avoid: songs (very different rhythm), fast anime action scenes, content with strong regional dialect.

## A 10-minute shadowing routine

- Minute 1: listen blind.
- Minute 2: listen with transcript.
- Minutes 3–4: parrot line-by-line.
- Minutes 5–7: full-clip shadowing (3× at least).
- Minute 8: record yourself on your phone and play it back.
- Minutes 9–10: re-shadow once more, focusing on whatever your recording revealed.

That last step (recording + playback) is humbling and essential. Your mouth thinks it's doing something it isn't.

## What to expect

- First week: slow, clumsy, mouth doesn't keep up.
- Third week: can follow simple dialogs in real time.
- By phase 2: noticeable improvement in your unscripted speaking rhythm.
