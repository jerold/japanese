# Daily Input Habits

Language learning compounds brutally well — a little every day beats a weekend binge every time. The aim of phase 1 is to build a habit loop that survives a bad week.

## The 15-minute floor

On low-energy days, do **just 15 minutes**. One daily lesson, nothing more. Consistency is the investment; intensity is optional.

On good days, add any of:
- An extra 10–15 minutes of listening input (see [../listening/suggested-resources.md](../listening/suggested-resources.md)).
- A few minutes of reading — even kana practice on product labels or signs.
- Shadowing (repeating aloud along with native audio).

## The three input modes

1. **Comprehensible input** — content just barely above your current level. This is where most learning happens. Children's shows, slow podcasts, NHK Easy.
2. **Ambient input** — too hard to fully understand, but keeps the ear exposed. Normal TV, anime, music. Not a substitute for comprehensible input, but useful ambient soak.
3. **Study input** — this project's daily lessons, vocab lists, grammar notes. Focused, structured.

A healthy phase-1 balance: 60% study input, 30% comprehensible input, 10% ambient.

## Anchor the habit

Tie Japanese to an existing daily moment:
- Morning coffee → today's lesson.
- Commute → passive listening.
- Before bed → 5 min vocab review.

The specific time matters less than the anchor. Don't negotiate with yourself; negotiate once, then just do it.

## Track streaks, forgive lapses

A simple checkbox on a paper calendar is enough. Miss a day? Next day resume. Miss three? Shorten the lesson — half is infinitely more than zero.

## What to avoid

- **App-hopping.** Trying every app in week one costs time you could spend learning.
- **Grammar-binging.** Reading five grammar articles without any practice creates an illusion of progress.
- **Perfectionism on kana writing.** Recognition first. Handwriting can wait.

## Weekly review

Every ~7 days, do a short review pass:
- Re-read yesterday-of-last-week's lesson.
- Read through your notes.
- Redo one quiz from earlier in the week.

This is your built-in spaced repetition — already partly baked into the daily quizzes.
