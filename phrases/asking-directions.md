# Asking Directions

Japanese addresses are notoriously opaque (the numbering isn't by street but by block within a ward), so asking a human is often the fastest path. The phrases below will get you oriented.

## The headline question

- **[place] はどこですか？** (~ wa doko desu ka?) = "Where is ~?"
  - 駅はどこですか？ = "Where's the station?"
  - トイレはどこですか？ = "Where's the bathroom?"
  - コンビニはどこですか？ = "Where's a convenience store?"

## More polite version

- **[place] はどちらですか？** = "Where is ~?"
  - どちら (dochira) is the polite form of どこ.

## Asking if a place is nearby

- この近くに[place] はありますか？ (kono chikaku ni ~ wa arimasu ka?) = "Is there a ~ nearby?"

## Understanding the answer

Key direction words:
- まっすぐ (massugu) = straight
- 右 (migi) = right
- 左 (hidari) = left
- 次の信号 (tsugi no shingou) = next traffic light
- 角 (kado) = corner

Common responses:
- まっすぐ行ってください。(massugu itte kudasai.) = "Please go straight."
- 次の信号を右に曲がってください。(tsugi no shingou wo migi ni magatte kudasai.) = "Turn right at the next light."
- あそこです。(asoko desu.) = "It's over there."
- すぐそこです。(sugu soko desu.) = "It's right there."

(The て-form + ください here is polite-request grammar — covered in phase 2. Memorize the shapes.)

## If you don't catch the answer

- **ゆっくりお願いします** (yukkuri onegai shimasu) = "Slowly, please."
- **もう一度お願いします** (mou ichido onegai shimasu) = "Once more, please."
- **すみません、わかりません** = "Sorry, I don't understand."

## Escape hatch

- 地図を見せてもらえますか？ = "Could you show me on a map?" — Or pull up Google Maps and point.
