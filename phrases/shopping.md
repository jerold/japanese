# Shopping

A small, repeatable script that covers you in most Japanese shops.

## Getting the staff's attention

- **すみません** (sumimasen) — "Excuse me." Safe opener.

## Asking where something is

- **[item] はどこですか？** (~ wa doko desu ka?) = "Where is ~?"
  - お水はどこですか？ — "Where is the water?"
  - トイレはどこですか？ — "Where is the bathroom?"

## Asking the price

- **これはいくらですか？** (kore wa ikura desu ka?) = "How much is this?"
- **全部でいくらですか？** (zenbu de ikura desu ka?) = "How much all together?"

Useful price words:
- 円 (en) = yen. 500円 = gohyaku-en. 1,000円 = sen-en.
- 高い (takai) = expensive. 安い (yasui) = cheap.

## Asking for something by pointing

- **これをください** (kore wo kudasai) = "This, please."
- **これ二つください** (kore futatsu kudasai) = "Two of these, please."
- **[item] はありますか？** (~ wa arimasu ka?) = "Do you have ~?"

## Negotiating: trying something on / asking for a different size

- 試着してもいいですか？ (shichaku shite mo ii desu ka?) = "May I try it on?"
- もっと大きいサイズはありますか？ = "Do you have a bigger size?"
- 色違いはありますか？ = "Do you have it in another color?"

(These use the ～てもいい pattern — phase 2 grammar. Memorize them as stock phrases for now.)

## Paying

- **カードで払えますか？** (kaado de haraemasu ka?) = "Can I pay by card?"
- **現金で** (genkin de) = "In cash."
- **袋お願いします** (fukuro onegai shimasu) = "A bag, please." Increasingly, these are paid — 3–10円.
- **レシートお願いします** = "Receipt, please."

## Thanks

Staff will bow and say:
- **ありがとうございました** (arigatou gozaimashita — note the past form for a completed service).
- **またどうぞお越しくださいませ** (mata douzo okoshi kudasaimase) = "Please come again." No response needed; a small nod is courteous.
