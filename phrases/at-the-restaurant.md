# At the Restaurant

Order of operations at a typical restaurant: be greeted, tell them party size, be seated, receive a menu, order (often by pressing a button or calling the server), eat, ask for the check, pay at the counter.

## Being welcomed

You'll hear **いらっしゃいませ** (irasshaimase) — "Welcome." You don't need to reply; a nod is fine.

## Saying party size

Staff will ask **何名様ですか？** (nan-mei-sama desu ka?) — "How many people?"

Answer with ～人 (people counter):
- 一人です (hitori desu) = "One person."
- 二人です (futari desu) = "Two people."
- 三人です (san-nin desu) = "Three people."

## Calling the server

In many casual restaurants you press a button; in others you say:
- **すみません！** (sumimasen!) — "Excuse me!" — perfectly polite here.

## Ordering

- **これをください** (kore wo kudasai) = "This one, please." (Pointing is fine.)
- **[item] をください** = "~, please." e.g., お水をください (mizu wo kudasai) = "Water, please."
- **[item] をお願いします** — a slightly more polite version.

### Numbers with drinks/dishes

- ビールを一つ (biiru wo hitotsu) = "One beer." Use the native counter ～つ for casual counting (see [numbers](../vocab/numbers.md)).
- コーヒーを二つ (koohii wo futatsu) = "Two coffees."

## Asking about the menu

- これは何ですか？ (kore wa nan desu ka?) = "What is this?"
- 辛いですか？ (karai desu ka?) = "Is it spicy?"
- おすすめは何ですか？ (osusume wa nan desu ka?) = "What do you recommend?"

## Food allergies / dietary needs

- **～が食べられません** (~ ga taberaremasen) = "I can't eat ~."
  - 肉が食べられません。(niku ga taberaremasen.) = "I can't eat meat."
- **ベジタリアンです** (bejitarian desu) = "I'm vegetarian."

## Enjoying and thanking

- **いただきます** (itadakimasu) — said before eating; "I humbly receive." A soft ritual acknowledgement of the meal.
- **ごちそうさまでした** (gochisousama deshita) — said after eating; "That was a feast." Said to the server or cook.

## Asking for the check

- **お会計お願いします** (okaikei onegai shimasu) = "The check, please."
- Or: **お勘定お願いします** (okanjou onegai shimasu) — same meaning.

Most places ask you to pay at a counter near the door, not at the table.
