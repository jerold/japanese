# Small Talk

Japanese small-talk gravitates toward weather, seasons, food, and work — and avoids deeply personal topics with strangers. A short exchange of these phrases is a warm, low-stakes way to connect.

## Weather

- **今日はいい天気ですね** (kyou wa ii tenki desu ne) = "Nice weather today, isn't it?"
- **暑いですね** (atsui desu ne) = "It's hot, isn't it?"
- **寒いですね** (samui desu ne) = "It's cold, isn't it?"
- **雨ですね** (ame desu ne) = "Raining, huh."

The **ね** at the end expects mild agreement — it's the grease of Japanese small-talk.

## How's it going

- **最近どうですか？** (saikin dou desu ka?) = "How have you been lately?"
- **お仕事はどうですか？** (oshigoto wa dou desu ka?) = "How's work?"

Safe answers:
- まあまあです (maa maa desu) = "So-so."
- 忙しいです (isogashii desu) = "Busy."
- 元気です (genki desu) = "I'm good."

## Food & drink talk

- **何が好きですか？** (nani ga suki desu ka?) = "What do you like?"
- **寿司が好きですか？** (sushi ga suki desu ka?) = "Do you like sushi?"
- **美味しいですね** (oishii desu ne) = "Delicious, isn't it?"

## Agreeing and reacting

- **そうですね** (sou desu ne) = "Yeah, right / that's true."
- **そうですか** (sou desu ka) = "Oh really / is that so." — a neutral acknowledgment, not quite agreement.
- **いいですね** (ii desu ne) = "That sounds nice."
- **本当ですか？** (hontou desu ka?) = "Really?"

## Gentle conversation enders

- **そろそろ失礼します** (sorosoro shitsurei shimasu) = "I should get going soon."
- **またお会いしましょう** (mata oai shimashou) = "Let's meet again."

## A small cultural note

Silence in Japanese conversation is more comfortable than in English. If there's a pause, you don't have to fill it instantly — "letting" a beat of silence pass is normal, even polite.
