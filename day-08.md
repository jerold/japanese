## 3 · Grammar concept: の particle (possessive / modifier)

の glues nouns together. The most common use is possession, but it works for any "X of Y / X's Y" relationship.

```
[Owner] の [Thing]
```

- 私の本 (watashi no hon) — "my book"
- 田中さんの車 (Tanaka-san no kuruma) — "Tanaka-san's car"
- 日本語の先生 (nihongo no sensei) — "Japanese-language teacher" *(not possession, but modification)*
- 東京の天気 (toukyou no tenki) — "Tokyo's weather"

You can chain の freely:
- 私の友達の家 — "my friend's house"

Full page: [grammar/particle-no.md](./grammar/particle-no.md).

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. これは私の本です。
2. 彼の車は新しいです。 (atarashii = new — you'll formally meet this on day 24)
3. 日本語の先生は田中先生です。
4. 十 + 八 = 十八 (juu tasu hachi wa juuhachi)

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "This is my book."
2. "His car is new."
3. "The Japanese-language teacher is Tanaka-sensei."
4. "Ten plus eight is eighteen."

</details>

## 5 · Speaking practice

Describe three things near you using の:
- これは私のコーヒーです。
- これは私の本です。
- それはあなたの [X] ですか？

Then count aloud from 1 to 10:
- いち・に・さん・よん・ご・ろく・なな・はち・きゅう・じゅう

## 6 · Quiz

1. Translate: 彼の名前は田中です。 (namae = name)
2. How do you say "my car"?
3. What's 七 (nana) spelled in kana?
4. What do you count as, 1 to 5: ___ ___ ___ ___ ___.
5. True or false: の can be chained.

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. "His name is Tanaka."
2. 私の車 (watashi no kuruma)
3. なな (also しち shichi)
4. いち に さん よん ご
5. True — 私の友達の家 ("my friend's house") is perfectly fine.

</details>
