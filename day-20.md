## 3 · Grammar concept: question word placement

Unlike English, Japanese question words stay in the position the answer would occupy. You don't front-load them.

- 明日、どこに行きますか？ — "Where are you going tomorrow?"
  - どこ sits where the destination would be.
- 何を食べましたか？ — "What did you eat?"
  - 何 sits where the object would be.
- いつ来ますか？ — "When are you coming?"

Particle compatibility:
- Place-question (どこ) keeps its normal particle: どこ**に** (dest), どこ**で** (action), どこ**から** (from).
- Thing-question (何 / 誰) keeps its normal particle: 何**を** (object), 誰**が** (subject), 誰**に** (to whom).

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. いつ日本に行きますか？
2. これはいくらですか？
3. どうしてですか？
4. 誰と行きますか？ (preview of と "with" — day 21)

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "When will you go to Japan?"
2. "How much is this?"
3. "Why (is that)?"
4. "Who will you go with?"

</details>

## 5 · Speaking practice

Imagine a shopping trip. Ask aloud:
- これはいくらですか？
- すみません、トイレはどこですか？
- それは何ですか？

And a curious conversation:
- いつ来ましたか？ — "When did you come?"
- どうしてですか？ — "Why is that?"

## 6 · Quiz

1. Translate: いくらですか？
2. Translate: いつ来ますか？
3. Why is どうして softer than なぜ in daily speech?
4. Where does a question word typically sit in a Japanese sentence?

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. "How much is it?"
2. "When are you coming?"
3. どうして feels warmer/more conversational; なぜ is more formal or written.
4. In the same slot the answer would occupy — it doesn't move to the front.

</details>
