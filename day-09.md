# Day 9 — Essential Verbs & the ~ます Form

**Target time:** 15–20 minutes.

Today's lesson is a turning point — with verbs, you leave "I am X" territory and start *doing* things in Japanese.

## 1 · New vocabulary (3)

| Kanji | Kana | Romaji | English | Polite form |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 行く | いく | iku | to go | 行きます (ikimasu) |
| 来る | くる | kuru | to come | 来ます (kimasu) |
| する | する | suru | to do | します (shimasu) |

Notes: 来る and する are the **only** irregular verbs in Japanese. 行く is mostly regular (group 1). See [vocab/essential-verbs.md](./vocab/essential-verbs.md).

## 3 · Grammar concept: the polite ~ます form

Use ~ます when you don't know someone well — strangers, shopkeepers, teachers, coworkers. It is Japan's default *public* verb form.

| | Affirmative | Negative |
|---|---|---|
| Non-past (now / future) | ～ます | ～ません |
| Past | ～ました | ～ませんでした |

We'll introduce the past and negative in later lessons. For now:

- 行きます (ikimasu) — "I go / I'll go"
- 来ます (kimasu) — "I / someone comes"
- します (shimasu) — "I do"

One ~ます form covers *both* present and future. Context decides. 行きます can mean "I go" or "I will go."

Full page: [grammar/masu-form-verbs.md](./grammar/masu-form-verbs.md).

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. 明日、学校に行きます。 (ashita = tomorrow, gakkou = school, に = direction marker)
2. 田中さんが来ます。
3. 私は勉強します。 (benkyou = study — 勉強する = "to study")
4. 何をしますか？ (nani wo shimasu ka?)

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "I'll go to school tomorrow."
2. "Tanaka-san will come."
3. "I study." (This is a する-verb: 勉強 + する.)
4. "What will you do?"

</details>

## 5 · Speaking practice

Say aloud what you're doing / going to do:
- 今、コーヒーを飲みます。 (ima, koohii wo nomimasu — "I'm drinking coffee now.") *(飲む "to drink" comes tomorrow — preview.)*
- 明日、学校に行きます。 / 明日、仕事に行きます。 (shigoto = work)
- 勉強します。

## 6 · Quiz

1. Convert to ~ます form: 行く → ___
2. What's unusual about 来る?
3. Translate: 私は明日、来ます。
4. Does ~ます mean "present" or "future"?
5. True or false: する is a regular verb.

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. 行きます (ikimasu)
2. It's irregular. The reading changes from `ku` in くる to `ki` in きます.
3. "I will come tomorrow."
4. Both — Japanese non-past ~ます covers present and future.
5. False — it's irregular.

</details>
