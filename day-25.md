# Day 25 — More i-Adjectives (new / old / expensive / cheap)

**Target time:** 15–20 minutes.

## 1 · New vocabulary (4)

| Kanji | Kana | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|---|
| 新しい | あたらしい | atarashii | new |
| 古い | ふるい | furui | old (for things) |
| 高い | たかい | takai | expensive / tall |
| 安い | やすい | yasui | cheap |

Detail: [vocab/adjectives.md](./vocab/adjectives.md).

⚠️ 高い has two meanings — "expensive" and "tall." Context decides. A tall building and a pricey watch can both be 高い.

⚠️ 古い describes *things*. For elderly people, don't use 古い — it reads as "worn out." Use 年上 (toshiue, "older") or similar.

## 3 · Grammar concept: i-adjective negatives in practice

You know how i-adjectives conjugate. Today let's use negatives at conversational speed.

Build these aloud:
- 新しい → 新しくないです (not new)
- 古い → 古くないです
- 高い → 高くないです
- 安い → 安くないです

Past:
- 高かったです。 — "It was expensive."
- 高くなかったです。 — "It wasn't expensive."

### Noun + i-adjective

- 新しい車 (new car)
- 古い本 (old book)
- 高いコーヒー (expensive coffee)

Full conjugation: [grammar/i-adjectives.md](./grammar/i-adjectives.md).

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. この車は新しいです。
2. その本は古くないです。
3. 高いコーヒーは美味しいです。 (oishii = delicious)
4. 昨日買ったパンは安かったです。 (katta = bought, plain past)

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "This car is new."
2. "That book isn't old."
3. "Expensive coffee is delicious."
4. "The bread I bought yesterday was cheap."

</details>

## 5 · Speaking practice

## 6 · Quiz

1. Negate: 新しい → ___
2. Past: 安い → ___
3. Translate: 高い車です。
4. Both meanings of 高い?
5. Translate: 古い本を読みます。

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. 新しくない (atarashikunai)
2. 安かった (yasukatta)
3. "It's an expensive car." (or "a tall car" — context!)
4. "Expensive" and "tall."
5. "I read an old book."

</details>
