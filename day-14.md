## 3 · Grammar concept: に vs で deep dive

This trip-ups every learner. Here's the clean rule:

| Particle | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **に** | Place where something **exists** | 駅**に**います。 (I'm at the station.) |
| **に** | **Destination** of motion | 駅**に**行きます。 (I'll go to the station.) |
| **に** | **Specific time** | 七時**に**起きます。 (I wake up at 7.) |
| **に** | Indirect object (recipient) | 友達**に**会います。 (I meet a friend.) |
| **で** | Place where an **action** happens | 駅**で**待ちます。 (I'll wait at the station.) |
| **で** | **Means** / instrument | 電車**で**行きます。 (I'll go by train.) |

Same noun (駅) can take either particle depending on what the verb does. Choose by asking: *am I marking existence/destination/time/recipient (に), or where the action happens / how it's done (で)?*

Full pages: [grammar/particle-ni.md](./grammar/particle-ni.md) · [grammar/particle-de.md](./grammar/particle-de.md).

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. 朝、コーヒーを飲みます。
2. 夜、家でテレビを見ます。
3. 七時に起きます。 (okimasu = wake up)
4. 図書館で本を読みます。
5. 学校に行きます。

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "I drink coffee in the morning."
2. "I watch TV at home at night."
3. "I wake up at 7."
4. "I read books at the library."
5. "I'll go to school."

</details>

## 5 · Speaking practice

Describe your morning and night routine in two sentences:
- 朝、 [activity using で or no particle if verb doesn't need a place].
- 夜、 [activity].

Examples:
- 朝、コーヒーを飲みます。
- 夜、家で本を読みます。

## 6 · Quiz

1. Translate: 学校で勉強します。 vs 学校に行きます。 What's the role of each particle?
2. Which particle: 七時 ___ 起きます。
3. Which particle: 電車 ___ 行きます。 ("by train")
4. What's "morning" / "night"?
5. Translate: 朝、テレビを見ます。

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. で marks where the action happens ("I study **at** school"); に marks the destination of motion ("I'll go **to** school").
2. に (specific time)
3. で (means / instrument)
4. 朝 / 夜
5. "I watch TV in the morning."

</details>
