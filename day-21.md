## 3 · Grammar concept: と — "and" (exhaustive) / "with"

と has two uses:

### 1. "And" — between nouns

と links nouns into a complete list:
- パンと水 — "bread and water"
- 田中さんと山田さん — "Tanaka-san and Yamada-san"

と is **exhaustive**: "A and B and that's the full list." For "among other things," you'll use や (phase 2).

### 2. "With" (a companion)

With a person, と means "together with":
- 友達**と**映画を見ます。 — "I watch a movie **with** a friend."
- 家族**と**旅行します。 — "I travel with my family." (kazoku = family)

Don't confuse the *companion* "with" (と) and the *instrument* "with" (で, as in 箸で食べる "eat with chopsticks").

Full page: [grammar/particle-to-mo.md](./grammar/particle-to-mo.md).

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. 私は友達と駅に行きます。
2. 家にテレビがあります。
3. 店で本を買いました。
4. 田中さんと山田さんは先生です。

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "I'll go to the station with a friend."
2. "There's a TV at home."
3. "I bought a book at the shop."
4. "Tanaka-san and Yamada-san are teachers."

</details>

## 5 · Speaking practice

Describe your day using place nouns and と:
- 今日、 [place] に行きます。
- [person] と [verb]ます。

Examples:
- 今日、友達と店に行きます。
- 明日、家族と映画を見ます。

## 6 · Quiz

1. Translate: パンと水
2. Translate: 友達と行きます。
3. "With chopsticks" — which particle?
4. How do you say "home" (your own home)?
5. Translate: 駅はどこですか？

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. "Bread and water"
2. "I go with a friend."
3. で (for instrument/means — 箸で)
4. 家 (ie / uchi)
5. "Where is the station?"

</details>
