## 3 · Grammar concept: spatial nouns with の

Unlike English prepositions, Japanese spatial words are **nouns**. You attach them to other nouns with の.

```
[reference noun] の [spatial noun]
```

- 机の上 (tsukue no ue) — "on top of the desk" (lit. "desk's top")
- ベッドの下 (beddo no shita) — "under the bed"
- 駅の右 (eki no migi) — "right of the station"
- 家の前 (ie no mae) — "in front of the house" (mae bonus)

Combine with a location particle (に for existence, で for action):
- 机の上に本があります。 — "There's a book on the desk."
- 駅の左で待ちます。 — "I'll wait on the left side of the station."

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. 机の上に本があります。 (aru preview — coming day 17)
2. 右に曲がってください。 (magatte kudasai = please turn — phrase; magaru = "turn")
3. コンビニの左にカフェがあります。 (konbini = convenience store)
4. 下を見ます。

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "There's a book on the desk."
2. "Please turn right."
3. "There's a café to the left of the convenience store."
4. "I look down."

</details>
