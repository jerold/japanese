# Demonstratives — The こ・そ・あ・ど Series

Japanese demonstratives sit on a four-position grid by **distance** (near speaker / near listener / far from both / question) and by **type** (thing, modifier, place, direction).

|  | Near me (こ) | Near you (そ) | Over there (あ) | Question (ど) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Thing (pronoun) | これ kore | それ sore | あれ are | どれ dore |
| Modifier (+noun) | この kono | その sono | あの ano | どの dono |
| Place | ここ koko | そこ soko | あそこ asoko | どこ doko |
| Direction / polite | こちら kochira | そちら sochira | あちら achira | どちら dochira |

## Thing — これ / それ / あれ

Stand alone as nouns:
- これは本です。(kore wa hon desu.) = "This is a book."
- それは何ですか？(sore wa nan desu ka?) = "What is that (you have)?"

## Modifier — この / その / あの + noun

Attach to a noun:
- この本 (kono hon) = "this book"
- その人 (sono hito) = "that person"

You **must** attach a noun — *kono* alone is ungrammatical. Use *kore* if you mean "this one."

## Place — ここ / そこ / あそこ

- ここはどこですか？(koko wa doko desu ka?) = "Where is here?" / "Where am I?"
- トイレはあそこです。= "The bathroom is over there."

## Direction / polite — こちら / そちら / あちら

Originally directional, now used as polite versions of これ/それ/あれ when referring to people or directions:
- こちらは田中さんです。(kochira wa Tanaka-san desu.) = "This is Tanaka-san." (introducing politely)
- あちらへどうぞ。= "This way, please." (gesturing toward over there)

## Conversational distance

The grid maps to *physical* distance only loosely — it also maps to the conversational position of an idea:
- そ = "the X you just mentioned" — even abstract ideas live near the listener once the listener brought them up.
- あ = "the X we both already know about" — the shared-context far-away.

You'll feel this with practice; for now, lean on the physical-distance reading.
