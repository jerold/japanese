# から and まで

Partners in range-marking: **から** = from (start), **まで** = until (end).

## から — "from"

### Origin in place or time

- 東京から来ました。(toukyou kara kimashita.) = "I came from Tokyo."
- 九時から始まります。(ku-ji kara hajimarimasu.) = "It starts at 9."

### "Because" (sentence-final)

Attached to the end of a clause, から means "because" — reasoning.
- 疲れたから、寝ます。(tsukareta kara, nemasu.) = "I'm tired, so I'll sleep."
- 忙しいから、行きません。(isogashii kara, ikimasen.) = "I'm busy, so I won't go."

This "because" usage is different from the "from" usage only in position: *after a clause* it means because; *after a noun* it means from.

## まで — "until / as far as"

### Endpoint in place or time

- 駅まで歩きます。(eki made arukimasu.) = "I'll walk as far as the station."
- 五時まで働きます。(go-ji made hatarakimasu.) = "I work until 5."

## The canonical pair

から and まで frequently appear together to bracket a range:
- 月曜日**から**金曜日**まで**働きます。= "I work from Monday through Friday."
- 東京**から**大阪**まで**新幹線で行きます。= "I go from Tokyo to Osaka by bullet train."
