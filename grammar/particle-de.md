# で — Location of Action & Means

で has two core jobs, both united by the idea of "the *context* in which something is done."

## 1. Location where an action happens

When a verb happens at a place, use で.
- 家で食べます。(ie de tabemasu.) = "I eat at home."
- 公園で遊びます。(kouen de asobimasu.) = "I play at the park."
- 学校で勉強します。(gakkou de benkyou shimasu.) = "I study at school."

### で vs に for places

- **で** = the place where an action is *performed*.
- **に** = the place where something *exists* (with ある / いる) or the *destination* of motion.

| Sentence | English | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 駅**に**います。 | "I'm at the station." | existence (いる) |
| 駅**で**待ちます。 | "I'll wait at the station." | action happening there |
| 東京**に**行きます。 | "I'll go to Tokyo." | destination |
| 東京**で**買いました。 | "I bought (it) in Tokyo." | action happening there |

## 2. Means / instrument

で also marks the means by which something is done — how, with what, or using what.

- 電車で行きます。(densha de ikimasu.) = "I'll go by train."
- 日本語で話します。(nihongo de hanashimasu.) = "I'll speak in Japanese."
- 箸で食べます。(hashi de tabemasu.) = "I eat with chopsticks."

## 3. (Bonus) Scope / extent

Occasionally, で marks a group or scope:
- 家族で旅行します。(kazoku de ryokou shimasu.) = "We travel as a family."
- 一人で行きます。(hitori de ikimasu.) = "I'll go alone."

## Mnemonic

**に** → *at / to* a point (static existence or destination)
**で** → *where* the action unfolds, or *by what means*
