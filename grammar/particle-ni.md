# に — Direction, Time, Location-of-Existence, Indirect Object

に is one of the busiest particles in Japanese. The good news: its uses all share a "pointed-at" feeling — you're aiming something *toward* a target, whether that's a place, a time, or a person.

## 1. Destination (with motion verbs)

```
[place] に 行く / 来る / 帰る
```

- 学校に行きます。(gakkou ni ikimasu.) = "I go to school."
- 家に帰ります。(ie ni kaerimasu.) = "I return home."

## 2. Time (at specific times)

に attaches to **specific** time points — clock times, days of the week, dates. Relative times like 今日 (today), 明日 (tomorrow), and 毎日 (every day) *don't* take に.

- 七時に起きます。(shichi-ji ni okimasu.) = "I wake up at 7."
- 月曜日に会います。(getsuyoubi ni aimasu.) = "I meet on Monday."
- 明日行きます。(ashita ikimasu.) = "I'll go tomorrow." (no に after 明日)

## 3. Location of existence (with ある / いる)

For *being / existing* somewhere, use に.

- 駅にあります。(eki ni arimasu.) = "It's at the station."
- 部屋に猫がいます。(heya ni neko ga imasu.) = "There's a cat in the room."

Contrast with で (see [particle-de](./particle-de.md)) which marks where an **action** happens.

## 4. Indirect object — "to (a person)"

For giving, teaching, sending, telling — anyone receiving something:
- 友達にプレゼントをあげます。(tomodachi ni purezento wo agemasu.) = "I give a present to my friend."
- 先生に聞きます。(sensei ni kikimasu.) = "I'll ask the teacher."

## 5. Frequency / per-unit

- 一日に三回 (ichi-nichi ni san-kai) = "three times per day"
- 一週間に一回 (isshuukan ni ikkai) = "once a week"

## Quick decision tree

Ask: *what is に doing in this sentence?*
- Pointing at a **destination** with a motion verb? → に
- Marking a **specific time**? → に
- Saying **where something exists**? → に
- Indicating **who receives** the action? → に

If none of those fit, you probably want a different particle.
