## The shape of a Japanese sentence

Unlike English's Subject-Verb-Object, Japanese is Subject-Object-Verb (SOV), and roles are marked by particles rather than position. Everything before the verb can usually be reordered without confusion, because the particles keep roles clear.

```
[Topic は] [Subject が] [Time に] [Place で] [Object を] verb.
```

Example:
> 私は月曜日に図書館で本を読みます。
> (watashi wa getsuyoubi ni toshokan de hon wo yomimasu)
> "On Monday, I read a book at the library."

Break it down:
- 私は — (topic) "as for me"
- 月曜日に — (time) "on Monday"
- 図書館で — (place of action) "at the library"
- 本を — (object) "a book"
- 読みます — "read"

You don't need every slot filled every time — Japanese is economical. Drop anything understood from context.
