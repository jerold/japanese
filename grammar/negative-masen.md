# ~ません — Polite Negative

To make a polite verb negative, swap **ます** for **ません**.

| Affirmative | Negative | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 食べます | 食べません | "don't eat / won't eat" |
| 行きます | 行きません | "don't go / won't go" |
| わかります | わかりません | "don't understand" |
| あります | ありません | "there isn't / don't have" (inanimate) |
| います | いません | "there isn't / not here" (animate) |

## Examples

- 肉を食べません。(niku wo tabemasen.) = "I don't eat meat."
- 日本語がわかりません。(nihongo ga wakarimasen.) = "I don't understand Japanese."
- 明日行きません。(ashita ikimasen.) = "I won't go tomorrow."

## Interaction with particles

In negative sentences, it's common to swap は in for が or を to emphasize the contrast:
- 肉は食べません。= "I don't eat *meat* (but I eat other things)."

## Useful stock phrases

- わかりません。(wakarimasen.) = "I don't understand." — use this freely, it's polite.
- 知りません。(shirimasen.) = "I don't know." — subtly different from わかりません.
- 大丈夫です。(daijoubu desu.) = "I'm fine / no thanks." — not literally negative, but the common polite "no."
