# と and も

Two small but high-value particles.

## と (to) — "and" / "with"

### "And" (exhaustive noun list)

と links nouns into a complete list:
- パンと水 (pan to mizu) = "bread and water"
- 田中さんと山田さん (Tanaka-san to Yamada-san) = "Tanaka-san and Yamada-san"

と is **exhaustive** — "A and B and that's the full list." For "A and B (among other things)" you'd use や (phase 2 territory).

### "With" (a companion)

With a person, と means "together with."
- 友達と映画を見ます。(tomodachi to eiga wo mimasu.) = "I'll watch a movie with a friend."
- 家族と旅行します。(kazoku to ryokou shimasu.) = "I'll travel with my family."

Distinguish this "with" (companion) from the "with" of instrument (chopsticks, train) which uses で.

## も (mo) — "also / too"

も replaces は or が when the meaning is "X also…"

- 私**も**学生です。(watashi mo gakusei desu.) = "I am also a student."
- これ**も**ください。(kore mo kudasai.) = "This one too, please."

Notice: when you want to say "X also," the も **replaces** the は/が; you don't write *私はも*.

### Stacking も

Two も in a sentence = "both X and Y (also)":
- パン**も**水**も**あります。= "There is both bread and water."

### Negative + も = "not any / neither"

- 何**も**食べません。(nani mo tabemasen.) = "I don't eat anything."
- 誰**も**来ませんでした。(dare mo kimasen deshita.) = "Nobody came."

This pairing of a question word + も + negative verb is an extremely common pattern.
