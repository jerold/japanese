# の — Possessive & Modifier

**の** links nouns. Its core job is to make one noun modify another — often, but not only, showing possession.

## Possession

```
[Owner] の [Thing]
```

- 私の本 (watashi no hon) = "my book"
- 田中さんの車 (Tanaka-san no kuruma) = "Tanaka-san's car"
- 友達の家 (tomodachi no ie) = "my friend's house"

## "Of" / descriptive relationships

の glues nouns together for any modifying relationship, not just ownership:

- 日本語の先生 (nihongo no sensei) = "Japanese-language teacher"
- 大学の学生 (daigaku no gakusei) = "university student"
- 東京の天気 (toukyou no tenki) = "Tokyo weather / weather in Tokyo"

## Chaining

You can chain の indefinitely:
- 私の友達の家 = "my friend's house" ("my friend's house" → "me の friend の house")
- 日本語の先生の本 = "the Japanese teacher's book"

## Dropping the final noun

When the final noun is obvious, drop it:
- これは私**の**です。(kore wa watashi no desu.) = "This one is mine."
- 赤い**の**ください。(akai no kudasai.) = "I'll take the red one." (Here の is acting as a pronoun "one.")

## の as a nominalizer (preview)

の can also turn a verb phrase into a noun — "the act of ~ing":
- 食べるのが好きです。(taberu no ga suki desu.) = "I like eating."

You'll revisit this in phase 2. For now, use の as the noun-linker.
