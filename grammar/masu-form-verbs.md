# ~ます Form — Polite Verb Endings

The **~ます** form is the polite present/future tense. Use it whenever you don't know someone well (strangers, shopkeepers, teachers, coworkers). Its forms:

| | Affirmative | Negative |
|---|---|---|
| Non-past | **～ます** (masu) | **～ません** (masen) |
| Past | **～ました** (mashita) | **～ませんでした** (masen deshita) |

One tense covers present and future — context tells you which. 食べます can mean "I eat" or "I will eat."

## Converting dictionary form to ~ます

### Group 1 (u-verbs) — most common

Swap the final -u vowel for -i, then add ます.

| Dictionary | Stem | ~ます |
|---|---|---|
| 書く (kaku) | 書き (kaki) | 書きます (kakimasu) |
| 読む (yomu) | 読み (yomi) | 読みます (yomimasu) |
| 話す (hanasu) | 話し (hanashi) | 話します (hanashimasu) |
| 飲む (nomu) | 飲み (nomi) | 飲みます (nomimasu) |
| 行く (iku) | 行き (iki) | 行きます (ikimasu) |
| 買う (kau) | 買い (kai) | 買います (kaimasu) |
| 待つ (matsu) | 待ち (machi) | 待ちます (machimasu) |

Notice -su becomes -shi, -tsu becomes -chi, -mu becomes -mi, etc. Japanese prefers the -i-row kana after certain consonants.

### Group 2 (ru-verbs)

Drop る, add ます.

| Dictionary | ~ます |
|---|---|
| 食べる (taberu) | 食べます (tabemasu) |
| 見る (miru) | 見ます (mimasu) |
| 寝る (neru) | 寝ます (nemasu) |
| 起きる (okiru) | 起きます (okimasu) |

### Irregulars

Only two:
- する (suru) → します (shimasu)
- 来る (kuru) → 来ます (kimasu)

## Worked examples

- 毎日本を読みます。(mainichi hon wo yomimasu.) = "I read a book every day."
- 明日学校に行きます。(ashita gakkou ni ikimasu.) = "I'll go to school tomorrow."
- 寿司を食べます。(sushi wo tabemasu.) = "I (will) eat sushi."
