# です — The Copula

**です** (desu) is the polite linking verb — roughly English "is / am / are." It ties nouns and na-adjectives to the rest of the sentence.

## Basic pattern

```
[Noun A] は [Noun B] です。
```

- 私は学生です。(watashi wa gakusei desu.) = "I am a student."
- これは本です。(kore wa hon desu.) = "This is a book."
- 田中さんは先生です。(Tanaka-san wa sensei desu.) = "Tanaka-san is a teacher."

## With na-adjectives

- 元気です。(genki desu.) = "I'm well / energetic."
- 静かです。(shizuka desu.) = "It's quiet."

## With i-adjectives

です follows i-adjectives for politeness only — the adjective does the real conjugation work.
- 大きいです。(ookii desu.) = "It's big."
- **Do not** write *ookii da* or combine desu into the adjective; just tack です on the end.

## Past: でした

- 学生でした。(gakusei deshita.) = "I was a student."

## Negative: じゃないです / ではありません

Two common negatives, roughly equivalent:
- 学生じゃないです。(gakusei ja nai desu.) — conversational.
- 学生ではありません。(gakusei de wa arimasen.) — more formal.

## Negative past: じゃなかったです

- 学生じゃなかったです。(gakusei ja nakatta desu.) = "I wasn't a student."

## Casual version: だ

In casual speech, です becomes **だ** (da), or often just drops entirely.
- 学生だ。(gakusei da.) = "I'm a student." (plain)
- 学生。(gakusei.) = "A student." — with rising intonation it becomes a question.

As a beginner, stick with です.

## What です is *not*

です is not a verb of existence. To say "there is a book" you use ある, not です. See [existence-aru-iru.md](./existence-aru-iru.md).
