# Day 1 — Your First Japanese

Welcome. Today is deliberately light: a tiny bit of the writing system, four greetings, and a reassurance that you've already taken the hardest step — showing up.

**Target time:** 15 minutes.

## 1 · Kana onboarding

Read the vowel and **k-row** of hiragana aloud twice today. Don't try to memorize; just say them.

- あ (a) · い (i) · う (u) · え (e) · お (o)
- か (ka) · き (ki) · く (ku) · け (ke) · こ (ko)

See the full grid any time: [vocab/kana.md — the gojūon grid](./vocab/kana.md#the-gojuon-grid).

## 2 · New vocabulary (4)

Today's words: time-of-day greetings + one farewell.

| Kana | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| おはようございます | ohayou gozaimasu | good morning |
| こんにちは | konnichiwa | hello / good afternoon |
| こんばんは | konbanwa | good evening |
| おやすみなさい | oyasumi nasai | good night |

Fun fact: the は in `こんにちは` and `こんばんは` is pronounced **wa**, not *ha*. You'll meet this little historical oddity again.

More detail: [vocab/greetings.md](./vocab/greetings.md).

## 3 · Grammar concept: the copula です (preview)

You'll meet です (desu) formally on day 3, but here's the teaser: です means "is / am / are" — a little tag that goes at the end of a sentence to make it polite.

- 学生です。 — "I am a student."
- 先生です。 — "(This person) is a teacher."

Don't worry about producing it yet. Just recognize the shape.

Full page: [grammar/desu-copula.md](./grammar/desu-copula.md).

## 4 · Reading practice

Say each aloud. Try to read the hiragana, then check the romaji.

1. おはようございます。
2. こんにちは。
3. こんばんは。
4. おやすみなさい。

## 5 · Speaking practice

Pick the greeting you'd use at the current time of day and say it out loud three times. Use it on a family member or pet if one is nearby. There's no pressure to be understood — you're building a motor habit.

## 6 · Quiz

Answer aloud or jot down answers; then check.

1. What greeting would you use at 8am?
2. What greeting would you use at 9pm as a farewell before bed?
3. True or false: は in `こんにちは` is pronounced *ha*.
4. Write (or say) the five vowels of the hiragana chart in order.
