## 3 · Grammar concept: questions with は …か (consolidation)

You've quietly been using the full pattern:

```
[Thing]は[description] ですか？
```

- これは本ですか？ → "Is this a book?"
- 田中さんは先生ですか？ → "Is Tanaka-san a teacher?"
- 今、何時ですか？ (ima, nan-ji desu ka?) → "What time is it now?" (preview of time)

And questions with question words often use が:
- 誰が学生ですか？ → "Who is a student?"
- 何がありますか？ → "What is there?" (preview of ある)

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. これは一です。 (kore wa ichi desu.)
2. 二 + 三 = 五 (ni tasu san wa go)
3. 私は二十五歳です。 (watashi wa ni-juu-go sai desu — "I am 25 years old")
4. 元気ですか？— はい、元気です。

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "This is a '1'."
2. "Two plus three is five."
3. "I am 25 years old." (We'll formally learn 歳 / 〜歳 later.)
4. "Are you well?" — "Yes, I'm well."

</details>

## 5 · Speaking practice

Count aloud from 1 to 5 three times, increasing speed each round:
- いち・に・さん・よん・ご

Then say your age using the pattern: 私は [number] 歳です。(Even if you don't know every number yet, numbers 21, 25, 30 are reachable — 二十一, 二十五, 三十.)

## 6 · Quiz — end-of-week

1. Translate: 私は元気です。
2. Which is preferred, shi or yon, when counting aloud? Why?
3. What does か at the end of a sentence do?
4. Write the kana for "one": ___
5. Translate: 彼はとても元気です。
6. True or false: は and が mean the same thing.

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. "I am well."
2. yon — because shi sounds like 死 ("death").
3. Turns the sentence into a question.
4. いち
5. "He is very energetic / well."
6. False — は marks a topic ("as for X"); が marks a subject / highlights new information.

</details>
