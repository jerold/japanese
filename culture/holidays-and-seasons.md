# Holidays & Seasons

Japanese conversation is soaked in seasonal awareness — small-talk references the current season more often than in English. Knowing the big seasonal markers and a few holidays gives you conversational traction all year.

## The four seasons (四季 shiki)

| Season | Kanji | Core associations |
|---|---|---|
| 春 (haru) | Spring | 桜 (sakura, cherry blossom), 花見 (hanami, flower-viewing), new school year |
| 夏 (natsu) | Summer | humidity, 花火 (hanabi, fireworks), 祭り (matsuri, festivals), cicadas |
| 秋 (aki) | Autumn | 紅葉 (kouyou, colored leaves), food: mushrooms, chestnuts, sweet potato |
| 冬 (fuyu) | Winter | New Year, snow (especially north), hot springs, nabe (hotpot) |

## Major holidays to know

### お正月 (oshougatsu) — New Year (Jan 1–3)

Japan's most important holiday. Families gather, shrines are visited for 初詣 (hatsumoude, first shrine visit), and traditional foods like おせち料理 and 雑煮 are eaten. Greetings:
- あけましておめでとうございます。(akemashite omedetou gozaimasu.) = "Happy New Year." (Only after Jan 1.)
- 今年もよろしくお願いします。(kotoshi mo yoroshiku onegai shimasu.) = "Please treat me well this year too."

### ゴールデンウィーク (Golden Week) — late April to early May

A cluster of national holidays. Popular travel week; everything is crowded.

### お盆 (obon) — mid-August

Ancestral spirits are welcomed home. Many people return to their hometowns; cities partially empty. Bon-odori (dance festivals) are iconic.

### 敬老の日 (keirou no hi) — Respect for the Aged Day, September

### 文化の日 (bunka no hi) — Culture Day, Nov 3

### 勤労感謝の日 (kinrou kansha no hi) — Labor Thanksgiving Day, Nov 23

### クリスマス (kurisumasu)

Imported, mostly romantic/commercial. Couples go out; KFC is a traditional (no really) Christmas dinner.

### 大晦日 (oomisoka) — New Year's Eve

Eat 年越しそば (toshikoshi soba, "year-crossing noodles"); watch 紅白歌合戦 (Kouhaku, the annual song contest) on NHK.

## Small seasonal greetings

- 春らしいですね。(haru rashii desu ne.) = "It feels like spring, doesn't it?"
- 暑い日が続きますね。(atsui hi ga tsuzukimasu ne.) = "The hot days keep coming, don't they."
- 寒くなりましたね。(samuku narimashita ne.) = "It's gotten cold, hasn't it."
