## 4 · Grammar concept: は vs が (the topic vs subject split)

You already use は. **が (ga)** is its partner — marking the grammatical subject, and highlighting new or important information.

Rules of thumb (the full version lives at [grammar/particle-wa-vs-ga.md](./grammar/particle-wa-vs-ga.md)):

1. **は** = "as for X" (topic). Used for what you're already talking about.
2. **が** = "X is the one" (subject, highlights new info). Often the answer to a question word, or an introduction of something new.

Compare:
- 彼**は**先生です。 — "He (whom we're discussing) is a teacher." *(topic)*
- 誰**が**先生ですか？ → 彼**が**先生です。 — "Who is a teacher?" → "**He** is a teacher." *(new info)*

Also note: question words (誰 who, 何 what) themselves take が.

## 5 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. 田中さんは元気です。
2. 今日はちょっと寒いです。 (samui = cold)
3. コーヒーはとても美味しいです。 (oishii = delicious)
4. 誰が先生ですか？

## 6 · Speaking practice

Answer aloud:
- お元気ですか？ (ogenki desu ka?) — "How are you?"
- コーヒーは好きですか？ (koohii wa suki desu ka?) — "Do you like coffee?" *(suki = liked, na-adjective)*

Suggested replies:
- はい、元気です。 / まあまあです (maa maa = so-so).
- はい、好きです。 / いいえ、あまり… (amari = "not really," softens).
