## 3 · Grammar concept: で particle (location of action)

で marks the **place where an action happens** — as opposed to a static location (which uses に).

```
[place] で [action]
```

- 家**で**食べます。 — "I eat at home."
- 学校**で**勉強します。 — "I study at school."
- 日本**で**働きます。 (hataraku = work, 働きます) — "I work in Japan."

The other major use of で is **means** — how you do something:
- 電車で行きます。 — "I go by train."
- 日本語で話します。 — "I speak in Japanese."

### で vs に — the split

| | で | に |
|---|---|---|
| Sense | place of **action** | place of **existence**, destination, time |
| Example | 家**で**食べます (eat at home) | 家**に**います (I'm at home) |

Full page: [grammar/particle-de.md](./grammar/particle-de.md).

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. 私は図書館で本を読みます。 (toshokan = library)
2. 彼は日本語で話します。
3. 家で映画を見ます。
4. カフェで手紙を書きます。 (tegami = letter; kafe = café)

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "I read books at the library."
2. "He speaks in Japanese."
3. "I watch movies at home."
4. "I write letters at the café."

</details>

## 5 · Speaking practice

- 家で [verb] ます。 (at home)
- カフェで [verb] ます。 (at a café)

Try using your new verbs: 話す, 読む, 書く, 見る, 聞く, 食べる, 飲む.

## 6 · Quiz

1. で marks: (a) existence, (b) location of action, (c) destination.
2. Translate: 私は家で食べます。
3. What's the polite form of 書く?
4. True or false: で can also mean "by means of."

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. (b) location of action (and also means — so both (b) and a secondary "means" sense).
2. "I eat at home."
3. 書きます (kakimasu)
4. True — 電車で行きます, 日本語で話します.

</details>
