# Minimum Pairs

Pairs of words that differ by only one sound — drilling them sharpens your ear and your mouth. Read each pair aloud five times slowly, then faster.

## Long vs short vowels

| Short | Long | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| おじさん (ojisan) uncle | おじいさん (ojiisan) grandfather | `i` length |
| おばさん (obasan) aunt | おばあさん (obaasan) grandmother | `a` length |
| ゆき (yuki) snow | ゆうき (yuuki) courage | `u` length |
| こうこう (koukou) high school | ここ (koko) here | `o` length in both syllables |
| ビル (biru) building | ビール (biiru) beer | `i` length, katakana |

## Single っ / doubled consonant

| Without っ | With っ | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| きて (kite) come | きって (kitte) stamp |
| かこ (kako) past | かっこ (kakko) parentheses |
| いて (ite) stay (te-form of iru) | いって (itte) go (te-form of iku) | critical in conversation |

## ん before various consonants

The final ん changes color depending on what follows:
- Before m/b/p: sounds like `m`. さんぽ (sanpo, "walk") sounds almost like "sam-po."
- Before t/d/n: sounds like `n`. こんにちは (konnichiwa).
- Before k/g: sounds like `ng`. にんき (ninki, "popularity").

Try: さんかい (sankai, "three floors") — the ん feels "ng-ish."

## Liquid consonants

Japanese /r/ is neither English r nor l — it's a light tap.

| らりるれろ (ra ri ru re ro) | Practice saying "butter" quickly; the "tt" is close to Japanese r. |

Drill row:
- らいおん (raion, lion), りんご (ringo, apple), るす (rusu, absence), れもん (remon, lemon), ろぼっと (robotto, robot).

## Pairs that learners confuse

| Pair | Gloss |
|---|---|
| くうき (kuuki) / ここ (koko) | air / here — practice u vs o |
| おと (oto) / おうと (outo) — vomit — oh no | |
| ち (chi) / つ (tsu) | not interchangeable |
| しゅ (shu) / す (su) | combined vs single mora |

## Using this page

Pick 3 pairs a day. Say each aloud five times slowly, five times fast. When you can hear and produce them without thinking, swap in new pairs.
