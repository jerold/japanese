# Suggested Listening Resources

Listening is where beginners gain the most per hour. The goal isn't to *understand* everything early — it's to let your ear tune to the rhythm, pitch, and frequent chunks of the language. Start with materials designed for learners, then drift toward native content.

Many of these are freely available online; search by name.

## Beginner-friendly (use from day 1)

- **NHK World Easy Japanese (やさしい日本語)** — free audio lessons for absolute beginners, native-recorded dialogs with English support.
- **JapanesePod101 — Absolute Beginner season** — long-running podcast, free tier is enough to start. Focus on the dialog replay.
- **Nihongo con Teppei for Beginners** — podcast entirely in slow, simple Japanese. No English. Best for once you have ~50 words under you.
- **Speak Japanese Naturally by Yuko** (YouTube) — short native-spoken videos with Japanese subtitles, aimed at learners.

## Low-intermediate (phase 2 onward)

- **NHK News Web Easy** (www3.nhk.or.jp/news/easy/) — news articles rewritten in simpler Japanese with furigana. Has audio.
- **Comprehensible Japanese** (YouTube channel) — extensive listening with visuals and no translation. Adjustable by level.
- **Nihongo Con Teppei (regular)** — same host, faster.

## Early native content to aim for

- Children's anime with clear speech: Doraemon, Chibi Maruko-chan, Crayon Shin-chan. Visual context helps enormously.
- Slice-of-life anime with everyday dialog: Yotsuba&! (manga), K-On!, Flying Witch.
- 〈NHK for School〉 (www.nhk.or.jp/school/) — educational programs made for Japanese kids. Clear diction, simple vocabulary.

## Passive vs active listening

- **Active listening** (15–20 min/day): full attention, replay confusing lines, write down 1–2 new phrases.
- **Passive listening** (anytime): background audio while commuting or doing dishes. Doesn't teach much alone, but keeps your ear wet.

Ratio roughly 70% active / 30% passive in phase 1.

## Shadowing

A specific technique: repeat aloud with minimal delay after the speaker. See [../immersion/shadowing-technique.md](../immersion/shadowing-technique.md).

## A practical daily routine

1. Pick one 2–5 minute segment at your level.
2. Listen once without looking at any text — just absorb the rhythm.
3. Listen again with the transcript.
4. Listen a third time and shadow aloud.
5. Note 1–2 new chunks in your own words.

Five minutes of this beats an hour of distracted listening.
