# Syllable Rhythm — the Mora

English is a **stress-timed** language: stressed syllables take roughly equal time regardless of how many unstressed syllables are between them. Japanese is **mora-timed**: every mora is approximately the same length.

## What counts as one mora

- Each basic kana = 1 mora. か = 1 mora.
- A long vowel adds a mora. おお = 2 morae.
- The final ん = 1 mora on its own.
- The small っ (silent pause before a consonant) = 1 mora of silence.
- Combined yōon like きゃ still count as 1 mora, despite being two characters.

## Counting practice

| Word | Morae | Beats |
|---|---|---|
| ねこ (neko) | 2 | ne-ko |
| さくら (sakura) | 3 | sa-ku-ra |
| にほん (nihon) | 3 | ni-ho-n |
| とうきょう (toukyou) | 4 | to-u-kyo-u |
| がっこう (gakkou) | 4 | ga-(っ)-ko-u |
| ほっかいどう (hokkaidou) | 5 | ho-(っ)-ka-i-do-u (you can feel the 6-ish length) |

## Why this matters

Native Japanese speakers can instantly tell when a word has the wrong mora count, even if you used the right phonemes. Compressing 東京 into two English-like syllables "toh-kyoh" loses the rhythm; holding it out to 4 beats as "to-u-kyo-u" reads as native.

## Drills

1. **Clap-count.** Clap once per mora as you say a word aloud. Start slow.
2. **Long vs short pair.** Practice adjacent pairs:
   - おじさん (ojisan, uncle, 4) vs おじいさん (ojiisan, grandfather, 5).
   - とる (toru, take, 2) vs とおる (tooru, pass through, 3).
   - まど (mado, window, 2) vs まっど? — the small っ changes timing drastically.
3. **Metronome.** Set a metronome to ~120 bpm and say one mora per beat.
