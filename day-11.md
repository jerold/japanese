## 3 · Grammar concept: consolidation — S は O を V

You now have the core Japanese sentence shape:

```
[Topic] は [Object] を [Verb]。
```

- 私はテレビを見ます。 — "I watch TV."
- 彼は音楽を聞きます。 (ongaku = music) — "He listens to music."
- 田中さんは本を読みます。 — "Tanaka-san reads books."

Everything before the verb can usually be reordered; the particles keep roles straight. You can also drop the topic entirely once it's established:

- テレビを見ますか？ — "Do (you) watch TV?"

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. 私は毎日テレビを見ます。 (mainichi = every day)
2. 音楽を聞きますか？
3. 先生に聞きます。 (to the teacher — note に, not を, for a person you're asking)
4. 彼女は映画を見ます。 (eiga = movie)

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "I watch TV every day."
2. "Do you listen to music?"
3. "I'll ask the teacher." (Notice: for *asking* someone, use に; for *listening to* something, use を.)
4. "She watches movies."

</details>

## 6 · Quiz

1. What two meanings does 聞く have?
2. Translate: 彼はパンを食べます。
3. In 先生に聞きます ("I'll ask the teacher"), why is に used instead of を?
4. What's the polite form of 見る?

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. "To listen" and "to ask."
2. "He eats bread."
3. Because a person you ask is an *indirect object* (marked with に), not something being acted on directly. You'll explore this on day 13/15.
4. 見ます (mimasu)

</details>
