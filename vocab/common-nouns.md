# Common Nouns

The everyday nouns that show up across food, transit, school, and basic introductions. Group them mentally by life-area to make them stick.

## Food & drink

### mizu

**水** (mizu) — "water." Always cold/room-temp water; if you want hot water it's お湯 (oyu).

### ocha

**お茶** (ocha) — "tea." The default in Japan is green tea. The お is honorific and effectively part of the word.

### gohan

**ご飯** (gohan) — "rice" or "meal" (cooked rice → meal by extension).
朝ごはん = breakfast, 昼ごはん = lunch, 夜ごはん = dinner.

## Places

### ie

**家** (ie) — "house / home." Also read うち (uchi) when meaning "my home."

### gakkou

**学校** (gakkou) — "school." (Note: long `ou` vowel — drag it out.)

### eki

**駅** (eki) — "(train) station." Indispensable in Japan.

### mise

**店** (mise) — "shop / store." Polite/honorific: お店 (omise).

## People

### hito

**人** (hito) — "person." Pluralized with たち: 人たち (hitotachi) = "people."

### tomodachi

**友達** (tomodachi) — "friend." Already plural-feeling; you don't usually add たち.

### sensei

**先生** (sensei) — "teacher / doctor / instructor / master."
A title of respect. Used for teachers, doctors, novelists, martial-arts instructors. Address them as `[name]-sensei`, never with さん.

### namae

**名前** (namae) — "name."
お名前は何ですか？ (onamae wa nan desu ka?) = "What is your name?"

## Things

### hon

**本** (hon) — "book."

### kuruma

**車** (kuruma) — "car."

### densha

**電車** (densha) — "(electric) train." Distinguished from 列車 (ressha, long-distance train) and 新幹線 (shinkansen, bullet train).
