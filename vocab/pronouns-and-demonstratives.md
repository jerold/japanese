# Pronouns & Demonstratives

Japanese drops pronouns far more often than English — once a topic is established, you rarely repeat it. Use these sparingly.

## Personal pronouns

### watashi

**私** (watashi) — "I" / "me."
Neutral, polite, works for any speaker. Men in casual settings often switch to **僕** (boku) or the rougher **俺** (ore); women may use **あたし** (atashi) casually. As a beginner, stay with `watashi`.

### anata

**あなた** (anata) — "you."
Use with caution. It can sound distant, presumptuous, or even aggressive. In practice, address people by their **name + さん** instead (e.g., 田中さん = "Tanaka-san"). Wives traditionally use あなた for husbands, which is one reason it sounds personal.

### kare

**彼** (kare) — "he" / "boyfriend."
Yes, the same word. Context disambiguates. Often replaced by name + さん.

### kanojo

**彼女** (kanojo) — "she" / "girlfriend."
Same dual meaning as `kare`.

### watashitachi

**私たち** (watashitachi) — "we" / "us."
The plural-marker `たち` (tachi) attaches to people-words to pluralize them: 子供たち (kodomotachi) = "children."

## Demonstratives — the こ・そ・あ・ど series

A clean four-way grid that maps to physical and conversational distance. Once you internalize this pattern you unlock many other words.

| | Near speaker | Near listener | Far from both | Question |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Thing | **これ** kore (this) | **それ** sore (that) | **あれ** are (that over there) | **どれ** dore (which) |
| Modifier | この kono | その sono | あの ano | どの dono |
| Place | ここ koko | そこ soko | あそこ asoko | どこ doko |
| Direction | こちら kochira | そちら sochira | あちら achira | どちら dochira |

### kore

**これ** (kore) — "this" (thing near me).
これは本です = "This is a book."

### sore

**それ** (sore) — "that" (thing near you, the listener).
それは何ですか？ = "What is that (you have)?"

### are

**あれ** (are) — "that over there" (far from both of us).
あれは富士山です = "That (in the distance) is Mt. Fuji."

### kono / sono / ano

These attach to a noun rather than standing alone:
- この本 (kono hon) = "this book"
- その人 (sono hito) = "that person (near you)"
- あの店 (ano mise) = "that shop (over there)"
