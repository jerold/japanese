# Adjectives

Japanese has two adjective types:
- **i-adjectives**: end in い in dictionary form. They conjugate themselves.
- **na-adjectives**: behave like nouns; need な before a noun and です to predicate.

For phase 1 we focus on i-adjectives. Full mechanics: [grammar/i-adjectives.md](../grammar/i-adjectives.md), [grammar/na-adjectives.md](../grammar/na-adjectives.md).

## ii

**いい** (ii) — "good."
Irregular: its negative and past forms come from the older form よい (yoi).
- Negative: よくない (yoku nai) = "not good."
- Past: よかった (yokatta) = "was good."
- いいですね (ii desu ne) = "Sounds good / Nice!" — very useful conversational reaction.

## warui

**悪い** (warui) — "bad."
- Negative: 悪くない (warukunai).

## ookii

**大きい** (ookii) — "big."
Note the long vowel — drag out the お sound.

## chiisai

**小さい** (chiisai) — "small."

## atarashii

**新しい** (atarashii) — "new."

## furui

**古い** (furui) — "old" (for things, not people — for elderly people use 年上 or another phrasing).

## takai

**高い** (takai) — "expensive" or "tall." Context disambiguates.

## yasui

**安い** (yasui) — "cheap / inexpensive."

## Two bonus very-common adjectives

Worth knowing even though they push us slightly over 100:
- 美味しい (oishii) = "delicious."
- 楽しい (tanoshii) = "fun / enjoyable."

## How they work

i-adjectives can sit in front of a noun directly:
- 大きい家 (ookii ie) = "a big house."

Or they can predicate with です (politeness only — です doesn't conjugate the adjective itself):
- この家は大きいです (kono ie wa ookii desu) = "This house is big."

Negative happens on the adjective, not on です:
- この家は大きくないです (ookikunai desu) = "This house is not big."
