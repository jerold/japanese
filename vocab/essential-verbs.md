# Essential Verbs

15 verbs that unlock most daily sentences. Every verb has a **dictionary form** (shown first) and a **polite -masu form** (used in most speech with strangers, coworkers, or anyone older).

Japanese verbs come in three groups:
- **Group 1 (u-verbs)**: end in a consonant + u sound (nomu, kaku, iku…)
- **Group 2 (ru-verbs)**: end in -iru or -eru with a consistent pattern (taberu, miru…)
- **Irregular**: only two — する and 来る.

Conjugation rules live in [grammar/masu-form-verbs.md](../grammar/masu-form-verbs.md). For now, memorize the pair.

## desu

**です** (desu) — "is / am / are" (copula).
Technically not a verb, but behaves like one. Attaches to nouns and na-adjectives.
- 学生です (gakusei desu) = "I'm a student."
- Past: でした (deshita). Negative: じゃないです (ja nai desu).

## aru

**ある** (aru) — "to exist / to be" (for inanimate things, and abstract things like plans).
Polite: あります (arimasu). Negative: ありません (arimasen).
- 本があります (hon ga arimasu) = "There is a book / I have a book."

## iru

**いる** (iru) — "to exist / to be" (for living things: people, animals).
Polite: います (imasu).
- 猫がいます (neko ga imasu) = "There's a cat."

Choosing `aru` vs `iru` is purely about animacy — a sleeping person still uses いる.

## iku

**行く** (iku) — "to go."
Polite: 行きます (ikimasu). Past: 行きました (ikimashita).
- 学校に行きます (gakkou ni ikimasu) = "I'm going to school."

## kuru

**来る** (kuru) — "to come." Irregular.
Polite: 来ます (kimasu). Past: 来ました (kimashita).
- 明日来ます (ashita kimasu) = "I'll come tomorrow."

## suru

**する** (suru) — "to do." Irregular, and the most productive verb in Japanese — many nouns become verbs by adding it (勉強する benkyou suru = "to study").
Polite: します (shimasu).

## taberu

**食べる** (taberu) — "to eat."
Polite: 食べます (tabemasu).
- 朝ごはんを食べます (asagohan wo tabemasu) = "I eat breakfast."

## nomu

**飲む** (nomu) — "to drink" (also used for "to take medicine").
Polite: 飲みます (nomimasu).

## miru

**見る** (miru) — "to see / to watch / to look at."
Polite: 見ます (mimasu).

## kiku

**聞く** (kiku) — "to listen" **or** "to ask." Context decides.
Polite: 聞きます (kikimasu).
- 音楽を聞きます (ongaku wo kikimasu) = "I listen to music."
- 先生に聞きます (sensei ni kikimasu) = "I'll ask the teacher."

## hanasu

**話す** (hanasu) — "to speak / to talk."
Polite: 話します (hanashimasu).
- 日本語を話します (nihongo wo hanashimasu) = "I speak Japanese."

## yomu

**読む** (yomu) — "to read."
Polite: 読みます (yomimasu).

## kaku

**書く** (kaku) — "to write."
Polite: 書きます (kakimasu).

## wakaru

**わかる** (wakaru) — "to understand / to know (something)." Technically takes が, not を:
- 日本語がわかります (nihongo ga wakarimasu) = "I understand Japanese."
Common in negative: わかりません (wakarimasen) = "I don't understand" — say this freely, it's polite.

## kau

**買う** (kau) — "to buy."
Polite: 買います (kaimasu).
- パンを買います (pan wo kaimasu) = "I'll buy bread."
