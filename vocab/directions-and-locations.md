# Directions & Locations

Relative directions in Japanese are often noun-like — they attach with の or に rather than functioning as prepositions.

## migi

**右** (migi) — "right."
駅の右 (eki no migi) = "right of the station."

## hidari

**左** (hidari) — "left."

## ue

**上** (ue) — "up / above / on top of."
机の上 (tsukue no ue) = "on top of the desk."

## shita

**下** (shita) — "down / below / under."
ベッドの下 (beddo no shita) = "under the bed."

## koko

**ここ** (koko) — "here."

## soko

**そこ** (soko) — "there."

## asoko

**あそこ** (asoko) — "over there."
Note the irregular form — you'd expect *`ako`* by the pattern, but it's `asoko`.

## doko

**どこ** (doko) — "where."
トイレはどこですか？ (toire wa doko desu ka?) = "Where is the bathroom?" (One of the most useful sentences you'll ever know.)

## Bonus positional words

Not counted in the 100, but closely related:
- 前 (mae) = "in front of" / "before"
- 後ろ (ushiro) = "behind"
- 中 (naka) = "inside"
- 外 (soto) = "outside"
- 近く (chikaku) = "near / nearby"
