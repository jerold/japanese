# Question Words

In Japanese, questions usually end with the particle **か** (ka) — a spoken question mark. Unlike English, the question word doesn't move to the front; it stays where the answer would go.

Example: "Where is the station?" → 駅はどこですか？ (eki wa **doko** desu ka?) — literally "station wa where is?"

## nani / nan

**何** (nani / nan) — "what."
Pronunciation flips based on what follows: `nani` before most words, `nan` before `d`, `t`, `n` sounds or a counter.
- 何ですか？ (nan desu ka?) = "What is it?"
- 何を食べますか？ (nani wo tabemasu ka?) = "What will you eat?"

## dare

**誰** (dare) — "who."
- 誰ですか？ (dare desu ka?) = "Who is it?"
- Polite/respectful form: どなた (donata).

## itsu

**いつ** (itsu) — "when."
- 誕生日はいつですか？ (tanjoubi wa itsu desu ka?) = "When is your birthday?"

## doko

**どこ** (doko) — "where."
Covered under [directions-and-locations](./directions-and-locations.md) too; it's doing double duty.

## naze / doushite

**なぜ / どうして** (naze / doushite) — "why."
`naze` is a touch more formal/written; `doushite` is warmer and more common in speech. Both are fine.

## dou

**どう** (dou) — "how / in what way."
- どうですか？ (dou desu ka?) = "How is it?" / "What do you think?"
- Not the same as どうやって (douyatte, "by what method") — that's for the procedural "how."

## ikura

**いくら** (ikura) — "how much" (cost/quantity).
- これはいくらですか？ (kore wa ikura desu ka?) = "How much is this?"

## Bonus — the doの family

You've already seen `doko` (where) and `dou` (how). The **ど** syllable is the "question" column of the `ko/so/a/do` grid (see [pronouns-and-demonstratives](./pronouns-and-demonstratives.md)). Other members:
- どれ (dore) = "which one"
- どの (dono) = "which [noun]"
- どちら (dochira) = "which way / which of two" (also polite "where")
