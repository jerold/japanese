# Time Words

The handful of time references that show up in nearly every conversation. Time words usually appear early in the sentence, before the topic.

## ima

**今** (ima) — "now."
今、何時ですか？ (ima, nan-ji desu ka?) = "What time is it now?"

## kyou

**今日** (kyou) — "today."
Read as `konnichi` only in formal compounds — in conversation it is always `kyou`.

## ashita

**明日** (ashita) — "tomorrow."
Also read **あす** (asu) in news/formal contexts. As a learner, stick with ashita.

## kinou

**昨日** (kinou) — "yesterday."

## asa

**朝** (asa) — "morning."
朝ごはん (asagohan) = "breakfast" (literally "morning rice").

## yoru

**夜** (yoru) — "night."
夜ごはん (yorugohan) or 夕ごはん (yuugohan) = "dinner."

## Bonus context (not counted in the 100)

You'll meet these constantly, even if we don't drill them yet:
- 午前 (gozen) = AM
- 午後 (gogo) = PM
- 今週 (konshuu) = this week
- 来週 (raishuu) = next week
- 先週 (senshuu) = last week
- 毎日 (mainichi) = every day
