# Phase 2 Outline — The Next 400 Words

Phase 1 gave you ~100 foundation words, polite ~ます conjugation, the copula, core particles, i- and na-adjectives, and situational scripts. Phase 2 triples that vocabulary and expands your grammar.

This file is a **scaffold**, not a curriculum. Each section names a theme, estimates a word-count target, lists seed items, and names the grammar points to layer in. Fill the detail when you're ready to do phase 2.

**Estimated length:** 40–50 daily lessons (days 29–78), ~15–20 min each. Target completion: ~7–10 weeks.

## Thematic vocabulary additions (~400 words)

- **Daily routine**: 起きる (wake up), 寝る (sleep), 立つ (stand), 座る (sit), 歩く (walk), 走る (run), 使う (use), 作る (make), 働く (work), 休む (rest), 笑う (laugh), 泣く (cry).

- **Communication**: 言う (say), 答える (answer), 電話する (call), メールする (email), 教える (teach), 習う (learn).
- **Possession & exchange**: 持つ (hold/have), あげる (give), もらう (receive), 貸す (lend), 借りる (borrow), 返す (return an item).
- **Perception & cognition**: 感じる (feel), 思う (think), 知る (know), 覚える (remember / memorize), 忘れる (forget).
- **Life**: 生まれる (be born), 死ぬ (die), 住む (live/reside), 結婚する (marry), 離婚する (divorce).
- **Cooking & food**: 料理する (cook), 切る (cut), 焼く (grill/bake), 茹でる (boil), 洗う (wash).

## Grammar points for phase 2

Layer these in across the 40–50 lessons:

1. **て-form** — the Swiss-army verb form. Used for requests (～てください), continuous states (～ています), connecting clauses, and more. Usually the biggest unlock of phase 2.
2. **Casual / plain verb forms** — dictionary form, plain past (食べた, 行った), plain negative (食べない), plain negative past (食べなかった).
3. **～たい (want to)** — 食べたい = "want to eat."
4. **Potential verbs** — 食べられる (can eat), 行ける (can go).
5. **Volitional** — 食べましょう (let's eat), 行こう (let's go).
6. **Conditionals** — ～たら, ～ば, ～なら, ～と.
7. **もう / まだ** — "already" / "not yet."
8. **〜から〜まで deep dive** — including clausal "because."
9. **から vs ので** — reasons, nuance differences.
10. **～が but** — mid-sentence contrast.
11. **～けど** casual "but."
12. **～ながら** — doing two things simultaneously.
13. **Giving and receiving** — あげる, くれる, もらう (famously tricky).
14. **Noun-modifying verb clauses** — 昨日買った本 ("the book I bought yesterday").
15. **Comparison** — ～より, 一番.
16. **Relative and absolute time reference** — この, その, あの for conversational time.
17. **Honorific and humble preview** — just recognition, not yet production.
18. **More counters** — ～枚, ～本, ～杯, ～匹, ～回.
19. **Quoting** — ～と言います, ～と思います.
20. **Politeness variation** — when to drop to plain form, when to lean more formal.

## Suggested lesson arc

- **Days 29–35**: Plain/casual verb forms + て-form introduction.
- **Days 36–42**: Requests (～てください), continuous (～ています), more verbs.
- **Days 43–49**: Days/months/dates + ～たい want.
- **Days 50–56**: Body & health + potential verbs.
- **Days 57–63**: Family, emotions + conditionals (～たら).
- **Days 64–70**: Transit, travel, work + giving/receiving verbs.
- **Days 71–78**: Comparison, quoting, noun-modifying clauses, capstone.

## How to know you're ready for phase 2

Rough self-check — you should be able to:
- Say the kana aloud without hesitation.
- Produce an introduction, ask for directions, and order at a café.
- Conjugate any ~ます verb you know to past / negative / negative-past without thinking too hard.
- Pick between は, が, を, に, で, の, と, も in most sentences correctly (not perfectly — nobody's perfect at は vs が).
- Read short self-contained hiragana-heavy sentences aloud and get most of the meaning.

If yes to most of the above, you're ready. If not, re-run a couple of phase-1 lessons rather than pushing forward.
