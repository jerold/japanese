# Day 3 — Finishing Hiragana & Your First Self-Introduction

**Target time:** 15–20 minutes.

## 1 · Kana onboarding

Last of the hiragana. Say each aloud once.

- は (ha) · ひ (hi) · ふ (fu, softer than English) · へ (he) · ほ (ho)
- ま (ma) · み (mi) · む (mu) · め (me) · も (mo)
- や (ya) · ゆ (yu) · よ (yo) ← no yi / ye slots
- ら (ra) · り (ri) · る (ru) · れ (re) · ろ (ro)
- わ (wa) · を (wo, pronounced "o")
- ん (n) ← the only standalone consonant

Two notes: **を** is used almost exclusively as the object particle (you'll see it on day 10). **ん** can never start a word; it always trails another kana.

Full grid + diacritics: [vocab/kana.md](./vocab/kana.md).

## 4 · Grammar concept: self-introduction template

Combine what you know:

```
はじめまして。
私は [name] です。
よろしくお願いします。
```

- `はじめまして` opens the introduction (only on first meeting).
- `私は [name] です` — "I am [name]."
  - は is the topic marker, pronounced **wa** (historical spelling).
  - です is the polite "is / am."
- `よろしくお願いします` closes the introduction.

More: [phrases/self-introduction.md](./phrases/self-introduction.md).

## 5 · Reading practice

Practice reading your own introduction aloud. Use a made-up name or your real one.

> はじめまして。私はジェロルドです。よろしくお願いします。
> (hajimemashite. watashi wa Jerorudo desu. yoroshiku onegai shimasu.)

## 6 · Speaking practice

Say your self-introduction out loud, start to finish, three times. Bow slightly at よろしくお願いします.

For pacing reference: [culture/bowing-and-greetings.md](./culture/bowing-and-greetings.md).

## 7 · Quiz

1. What's the opening word of a first-time introduction?
2. What does 私 mean?
3. Why is は pronounced "wa" in 私は?
4. Say aloud: はひふへほ. What's unusual about ふ?

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. はじめまして
2. "I / me" (watashi)
3. Because は is acting as the topic particle, which is historically pronounced "wa."
4. ふ (fu) is made with both lips, softer than English "f" — almost like blowing out a candle.

</details>
