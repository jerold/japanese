## 3 · Grammar concept: the direct-object particle を

を marks the **direct object** — the thing the verb acts on. Pronounced simply `o` (the w is silent in modern Japanese).

```
[Subject は] [Object を] [verb]。
```

- パン**を**食べます。 — "I eat bread."
- 水**を**飲みます。 — "I drink water."
- 本**を**読みます。 — "I read a book." (reading verb comes day 12)

Full page: [grammar/particle-wo.md](./grammar/particle-wo.md).

⚠️ A few English-transitive verbs use が instead of を in Japanese — we'll meet them later (e.g., 日本語**が**わかります "I understand Japanese"). Memorize them as exceptions when they come.

## 4 · Reading practice

Read aloud and translate:

1. 私はご飯を食べます。
2. 田中さんはお茶を飲みます。 (ocha = tea)
3. 明日、パンを買います。 (kau = buy, 買います → kaimasu; we'll cover it on day 15)
4. 何を食べますか？

<details><summary>Translations</summary>

1. "I eat rice / a meal."
2. "Tanaka-san drinks tea."
3. "Tomorrow, I'll buy bread."
4. "What will you eat?"

</details>

## 5 · Speaking practice

Describe what you ate/drank today. Pick from:
- コーヒー (coffee), お茶 (tea), 水 (water), パン, ご飯.

Pattern:
- 私は[item]を食べます / 飲みます。

For a meal you've already had, you'll need the past tense — you'll meet it formally on day 19. Use the present for now: 今日、パンを食べます。

## 6 · Quiz

1. Which particle marks the direct object?
2. Translate: 私はパンを食べます。
3. What's the polite form of 飲む?
4. How do you say "rice / meal"?
5. Pronunciation check: how is を pronounced?

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. を
2. "I eat bread."
3. 飲みます (nomimasu)
4. ご飯 (gohan)
5. Simply "o" — the w is silent.

</details>
